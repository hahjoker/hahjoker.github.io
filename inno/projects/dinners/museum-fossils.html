COURSE 01 — AMBER

Golden Beets, Parmesan Crisp, Citrus

PAIRING

Tirriddis Brut Rosé N.V. · Columbia Valley

dry, low residual sugar — a crisp, acid-driven opener.

méthode traditionnelle — the same secondary-fermentation-in-the-bottle process as Champagne. The winery's name literally spells out the three steps: tirage, riddle, disgorge. Made in Prosser, WA, one of Washington's oldest wine regions.

02
specimen #007.

COURSE 02 — SPECIMEN #007

Scallops, Caviar, Cucumber-Melon Agar

PAIRING

Orr Old Vine Chenin Blanc 2025 · Columbia Valley

honeydew melon, pear, straw, wet stone minerality, crisp acid.

Old-vine Chenin is genuinely rare in Washington — most plantings were ripped out decades ago in favor of Chardonnay and Riesling, so old vines here are a bit of a time capsule. Made by a female winemaker.

03
lunchbox.

COURSE 03 — LUNCHBOX

Potato Croquette, Stuffed Shishito, Artichoke

PAIRING

DeLille Cellars Chaleur Blanc 30th Vintage, 2024 · Woodinville

gooseberry, green apple, lemon brioche.

DeLille is one of Washington's founding "cult" wineries, established in 1992. Chaleur Blanc is a Bordeaux-style white blend — Sauvignon Blanc-forward, with Sémillon for texture.

04
the dig site.

COURSE 04 — THE DIG SITE

Chicken Ballotine, Panko, Mushroom Soil

PAIRING

Hightower Cellars Red Mountain Reserve 2022 · Red Mountain

83% Cabernet / 17% Malbec — dark blueberry, cherry, cocoa, tobacco, firm tannins. Decant it if you can, it opens up beautifully.

Husband-and-wife team Tim and Kelly Hightower founded this in 1997 with a simple motto: "get the best grapes and don't screw them up." They bought their Red Mountain land in 2002 from Sonny and Zeta Moore — the very first people to ever live there — and converted an old horse barn into the winery.

05
relics.

COURSE 05 — RELICS

Dark Chocolate Cake, 50% Chocolate Bones, Raspberry Coulis

PAIRING

Cerdon Renardat-Fâche “Initiale”

raspberry.

From Bugey, a small, lesser-known appellation in eastern France near Savoie. Made méthode ancestrale — the original way of making sparkling wine, older than Champagne's method.
